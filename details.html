ANDREW NIGHTINGALE / POETRY

Ten Clouds

Online pamphlet Red Ceilings Press (2011)

Concrete poems, first published in The Text (a part was also included in the Flarestack anthology Mr Barton Isn’t Paying), based on ten types of cloud with a poem for each type. It forms a narrative in ten poems about an untranslatable book, a Venetian secret society and a Hungarian mathematician-cum-swordsman, all mixed up with some experimental music, a Thai bride and a little martial art. The nomenclature of each cloud dictates the characters that appear in each poem.

‘His poetry is engaging and perplexing and enjoyable. It tells you a deep dark secret and then ensures that you immediately forget what you heard.’ Andrew Jordan, 10th Muse, 2008 (referring to first publication in The Text)

Hermegasmica

Online pamphlet Hermegasmica (2011)

A non-linear murder mystery set in Manchester. Meet Billie Holiday, Harry Haller (from Hesse’s Steppenwolf) and others on the night of the ‘Hermegasmica’ when a shop entrance briefly appears in an old brick wall…

‘I will admit that I haven’t unlocked the puzzle yet, but it’s been fun trying. When you’re feeling a little disorientated, the details Nightingale uses to describe character and setting are more than enough to keep you engaged.’ Poetry after Ink, March 2011

Talismanic Contact

Pamphlet Knives Forks and Spoons Press (2010)

A series of six visual poems.

‘They hover between the illusion of scientific remoteness and the daredevilry of poetry.’ Sabotage Reviews, November 2011

The Black Madonna

Pamphlet Original Plus (2010)

Five poems that explore aspects of the lives and myths of Mata Hari (previously published in Obsessed with Pipework), Ada Lovelace (previously published in Tremblestone), Lou Andreas-Salomé (previously published online in Poems Niederngasse), Isabelle Eberhardt and The Black Madonna (previously published online in Big Bridge) in a variety of forms, narrative and surreal, concrete and ekphrastic, lyrical and cut up.

The Big Wheel

Collection Oversteps Books (2009)

This collection includes visual poems, a poem made from poker dice permutations, a sequence of prose poems for fairground rides, and a poem about Katherine Mansfield.

‘The poet has a questing quizzical imagination, exploring worlds of outer and inner reality, and their liminal zones with remarkable force, insight and passion – an achieved and individual voice.’ Penelope Shuttle (back cover)

‘A collection of unusual scope in theme and imagery, but also in technique… The poems range from the surreal to the micro-drama and include prose poems as well as some luminously beautiful lyric poems.’ Judy Gahagan (back cover)

‘Nightingale has a deft hand… These lyrical short poems are quite brilliant… Like the best poetry Nightingale’s work enthrals, challenges and takes risks. This book comes heartily recommended.’ The Journal, Winter 2009/10
